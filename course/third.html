Gef--the giver
Hörn--flaxen
Mardöll--sea-brightener
Skjálf--shaker
Sýr--sow
Thröng--throng
Thrungva--throng
Valfreyja--lady of the slain
Vanadís--the dís of the vanir

Freya was the arrestingly beautiful Norse goddess of blessings, love, and fertility. A member of the Vanir tribe known for her skill in divination, she could be both a gentle ruler and a fierce warrior.

Many days of the week in English get their roots from Norse mythology, and Friday is commonly thought to be a portmanteau of “Freya’s day.”

Freya was married to the mysterious god Odr, possibly an alternative name for Odin, with whom she had two daughters, Hnoss and Gersemi.

Freya possessed a beautiful golden necklace known as the Brisingamen, which she famously bargained for from four dwarf craftsmen.
